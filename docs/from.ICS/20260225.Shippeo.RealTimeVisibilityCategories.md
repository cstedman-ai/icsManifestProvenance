## Category Explanations

### Multi-System Connectivity
The ability to connect and exchange data across multiple enterprise systems (ERP, WMS, TMS, OMS) without requiring a single monolithic platform. Real-time visibility acts as a unifying layer, pulling and pushing data between systems like NetSuite, warehouse management tools, and procurement platforms so that all stakeholders operate from the same source of truth.

### Direct Carrier Integration
A direct API or EDI connection to carrier networks (FedEx, UPS, freight carriers, LTL providers) that feeds shipment status updates into the visibility platform automatically. This eliminates the need to log into individual carrier portals and consolidates tracking across all shipping partners into a single view.

### Manual Data Entry
Support for human-entered data when automated feeds are unavailable. This covers scenarios where a warehouse associate manually logs a receipt, a vendor phones in a shipment update, or a carrier lacks API support. The platform accommodates these inputs alongside automated data without breaking the tracking chain.

### Bulk File Uploads
The ability to ingest large datasets via file upload (CSV, Excel, EDI flat files) for scenarios where real-time API integration isn't practical. The caveat "depends on preferred method" reflects that file format, encoding, and field mapping vary by organization and may require configuration.

### SKU-Level Detail Management
Tracking granularity down to the individual SKU (Stock Keeping Unit). Rather than knowing only that "a shipment is in transit," the system tracks which specific parts, models, or components are in that shipment — enabling item-level receiving, inventory accuracy, and discrepancy detection at the product level.

### Location Information Management
Capture and management of physical location data for shipments, warehouses, docks, and staging areas. This includes the ability to associate shipments with specific facility addresses, dock doors, or storage zones, and to update location context as goods move through the supply chain.

### PO-Level Tracking
Tracking shipments and receipts at the Purchase Order level, linking carrier movements and delivery events back to the originating PO. This enables procurement teams to see the fulfillment status of each order without cross-referencing separate systems — a PO issued in NetSuite can be tracked from vendor dispatch through warehouse receipt.

### Equipment-Specific Tracking
Visibility into specific physical assets such as containers, trailers, pallets, or racks. For hardware-intensive operations (servers, GPUs, networking equipment), this means tracking not just the shipment but the individual equipment unit — serial numbers, asset tags, and which container or pallet a specific piece of equipment is on.

### Real-Time Data Ingestion
The platform continuously ingests incoming data streams (carrier updates, GPS pings, scan events, API payloads) as they occur, rather than relying on batch processing or periodic polling. Events appear in the system within seconds or minutes of occurrence, enabling immediate action on delays, exceptions, or arrivals.

### Configurable Data Updates
The ability to customize what data is captured, how often it refreshes, and what triggers notifications. Different stakeholders may need different update frequencies or event types — warehouse managers may want dock-arrival alerts, while procurement may want milestone summaries. Configurability ensures each role sees what matters to them.

### Position and GPS Tracking
Live geographic positioning of shipments in transit using GPS data from carrier devices, truck-mounted units, or IoT sensors. This provides a map-level view of where goods are at any moment, estimated time of arrival calculations, and geofence-triggered alerts when shipments enter or leave defined zones.

### Milestone and Status Tracking
Tracking shipments against defined milestones in the delivery lifecycle: order placed, picked up, in transit, out for delivery, delivered, received. Each milestone is timestamped and attributed, creating a chronological audit trail. Status exceptions (delayed, held, returned) are surfaced immediately for resolution.

### Documentation Management
Association and management of shipping documents (bills of lading, packing slips, commercial invoices, certificates of origin, proof of delivery) with their corresponding shipments. The conditional note reflects that coverage depends on document format — a structured EDI document is simpler to ingest than a scanned PDF requiring OCR.

### Bidirectional Communication
Two-way data flow between the visibility platform and connected systems or partners. Rather than passively receiving updates, the platform can send information back — confirming receipt to a carrier, pushing status updates to a vendor portal, or triggering workflows in an ERP. This closes the communication loop between all parties.

### Inbound Vehicle Tracking
Specific tracking of vehicles (trucks, vans, freight containers) approaching a receiving facility. This enables dock scheduling, labor planning, and yard management by knowing not just that a shipment is "out for delivery" but where the vehicle is and when it will arrive at the gate.

### Pre-Pickup Visibility
Insight into shipment readiness before the carrier picks it up. This covers vendor-side events: has the order been packed, is it staged for pickup, has the carrier been notified? Pre-pickup visibility shifts the tracking window earlier in the process, catching delays before the shipment even enters transit.

### Multimodal Tracking
Continuous tracking across different transportation modes — ocean, air, rail, truck, last-mile delivery — within a single shipment lifecycle. A server chassis manufactured overseas might travel by ocean freight, transfer to rail, then truck to the warehouse. Multimodal tracking maintains visibility across all mode changes without gaps.

### Multi-Leg Data Linking
Connecting data across multiple legs of a shipment journey. When goods transfer between carriers, vehicles, or facilities (e.g., from a freight forwarder to a regional carrier to a final-mile driver), multi-leg linking ensures the entire journey is treated as one continuous tracking record rather than disconnected segments.

### Brokered Shipment Visibility
Tracking shipments that move through freight brokers or third-party logistics (3PL) intermediaries. Brokered loads add a layer of indirection — the shipper contracts with a broker, who contracts with a carrier. Visibility must penetrate through the broker layer to surface the actual carrier and real-time status of the underlying shipment.

### Freight Forwarder Integration
Direct connection to freight forwarders who coordinate complex, often international, shipments across multiple carriers and modes. Forwarders manage customs, consolidation, and routing. Integration ensures that the visibility platform receives updates from the forwarder's systems, maintaining tracking continuity for shipments the organization doesn't directly control.
